Linear Dilation
Linear dilation takes into account the dilation suffered by a body in only one of its dimensions.





Superficial Dilation
Surface dilation takes into account the dilation undergone by a given surface.




The coefficient of superficial expansion (β) is equal to to twice the value of the linear expansion coefficient (α), i.e.: β = 2 . α


Volumetric Dilation
Volumetric dilation results from the increase in the volume of a body.




The volumetric expansion coefficient (γ) is three times greater than the coefficient of linear expansion (α), i.e.: γ = 3 . α

First Law of Thermodynamics
The First Law of Thermodynamics relates to the principle of conservation of energy. This means that the energy in a system can neither be destroyed nor created, only transformed.




Second Law of Thermodynamics
Heat transfers always occur from the warmer body to the cooler body, this happens spontaneously, but not the other way around. Which is to say that the transfer processes of thermal energy are irreversible.
